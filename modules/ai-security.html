Module Overview
Why This Module Matters
Why You Need This
  • AI security incidents are rising — prompt injection attacks are now in OWASP's Top 10 for LLM apps
  • Regulators globally (EU AI Act, US EO) now require AI systems to have safety and governance
  • Data privacy in AI is a legal requirement — GDPR, CCPA apply to AI-processed data
  • Every company deploying AI is hiring for AI safety and governance roles
  • Engineers who understand AI risk differentiate themselves at every level of seniority
Learning Outcomes
  • Identify and defend against prompt injection and jailbreaking attacks
  • Implement input/output sanitization for production AI apps
  • Apply data privacy principles to AI pipelines (GDPR/CCPA basics)
  • Understand AI governance frameworks and responsible AI principles
  • Secure API keys, implement rate limiting and prevent data leakage
  • Evaluate AI outputs for bias, hallucinations and safety violations

For Trainers
Delivery Guide — How to Teach This Module

Recommended teaching flow, classroom techniques and assessment approach for trainers.

01 · Before Class
Pre-Class Preparation
  • Set up a sandboxed AI app from Module 6 for learners to attack ethically
  • Print the OWASP LLM Top 10 one-pager for every learner as a reference card
  • Install Presidio, Garak and Fairlearn on the demo machine and test imports
  • Prepare 5 worked prompt-injection examples that fail in clear, teachable ways
02 · In Class
Live Delivery Style
  • Open with a real prompt-injection demo — break a deployed AI on stage in 60 seconds
  • Always pair every attack with the corresponding defence — never teach offence alone
  • Make AI policy concrete by mapping every clause to a real engineering decision
  • Discuss recent real-world AI incidents from the news to keep urgency high
03 · Engagement
Student Engagement Tactics
  • Run a "Red Team / Blue Team" exercise — half attack the bot, half defend it
  • Award the most creative jailbreak each session — ethical bounty mindset
  • Discuss real-world AI incidents and have learners propose what they would have done
  • Hold a class debate on EU AI Act risk categorization with real product examples
04 · Assessment
Assessment Approach
  • Grade the security audit report on professional structure — CVSS scoring, remediation, evidence
  • Evaluate the secure wrapper on real attack resistance, not just code style
  • Test bias-audit reports for methodology, sample size and remediation clarity
  • Run a live "find the vulnerability" assessment where learners audit a fresh AI app
